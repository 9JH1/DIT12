Why dogs are the inferior species 12/12/1212
From the lofty perch of a cat's superior intellect, dogs are simply bumbling oafs who think drooling is a personality trait. While we felines glide with the grace of a moonlit shadow, dogs galumph around like furry wrecking balls, barking at their own reflections as if they've discovered a new species. They’re obsessed with fetching sticks—honestly, why chase something you didn’t even want in the first place? Meanwhile, we cats mastermind our domain, plotting naps and laser-pointer conquests with unmatched finesse. Dogs might have loyalty, but we have dignity, and that’s the purr-fect reason they’ll always be second-rate.
